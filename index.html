1916

Fueled by the Jim Crow southern states, “The Great Migration” occurs with many Black migrants settling in South, West, and North Philadelphia.

1944

Sister Rosetta Tharpe releases her song “Strange Things Happening Every Day”. This song is widely considered the first rock and roll single. Additionally, Patti LaBelle (born Patricia Louise Holte) was born in West Philadelphia.

1957

Sister Rosetta Tharpe moved to 1102 Master Street in Philadelphia.

1965

Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. joined civil rights activist Cecil B. Moore to speak to a massive crowd of demonstrators at Girard College.

1967

Gamble and Huff created "Expressway to Your Heart" with the Soul Survivors

1968

Frank Rizzo serves as Philadelphia police commissioner and The Electric Factory opens.

Joseph Tarsia opened the doors of Sigma Sound Studios, a state-of-the-art recording studio that ushered in a new era of Philly Soul.

1970

The first black musicians rehearse in Philadelphia Orchestra.

1971

Philadelphia International Records was founded by the writer-producer duo Kenneth Gamble and Leon Huff.

1972

Frank Rizzo's controversial tenue as mayor begins.

1976

Gamble and Huff produced “The Sound of Philadelphia” for the African American TV dance show Soul Train and became a signature style for the label. The singing parts on TSOP were performed by the vocal trio The Three Degrees. The song shortly became the first television theme song to hit number one on the Billboard Top 100.

1979

Lady B, one of the first female hip hop artists, released "To The Beat Y'all", putting Philadelphia on the hip-hop map.

1982

Philadelphia native and R&B star Teddy Pendergrass becomes paralyzed from the chest down. He was convinced to perform again in Live Aid in 1985.

1985

Live Aid in Philadelphia is organized, largely regarded as the largest and most successful concert to ever happen.

Schoolly D also releases his hit single “P.SK. (What Does it mean?)”, launching him to the forefront of what would later be dubbed “gangsta rap”.

1986

West Philadelphian MC Breeze released “Discombobulatorbublator”, a song banned for racist lyrics about Asians and Chinese food.

2000

Larry Magic and Allen Spivak sold the Electric Factory to SFX Entertainment.

2010

Conjunto Philadelphia is founded by Jeff Torchon in honor of the music on the Buena Vista Social Club album. Playing pre-revolutionary Cuban music, the band focuses on styles like Bolero, Cha Cha Cha, and Son.

2020

The Philadelphia Orchestra adds its first Black woman musician.
